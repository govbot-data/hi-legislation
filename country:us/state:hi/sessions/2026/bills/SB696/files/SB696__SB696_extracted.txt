BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that the absence of tax
2 incentives for home fortification, grants for low-income
3 families to strengthen their homes, and efforts to create and
4 enhance hurricane shelter capacity stems from a lack of a
5 unified approach to securing funds and dedicated personnel to
6 plan and manage such initiatives. Currently, the Hawaii
7 emergency management agency is primarily focused on post­
8 disaster response efforts for events such as hurricanes,
9 tsunamis, or fires. The legislature believes that the State has
10 an obligation to provide sufficient funding and resources to
11 ensure that residents of the State are thoroughly prepared for a
12 natural disaster.
13 Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to appropriate
14 funds for the office of the governor, in collaboration with the
15 insurance division of the department of commerce and consumer
16 affairs, to:
17 (1) Plan and administer:

S.B. NO. 646
Report Title:
Office of the Governor; DCCA; Disaster Preparedness;
Appropriation
Description:
Appropriates funds for the Office of the Governor, in
collaboration with the Insurance Division of the Department of
Commerce and Consumer Affairs, to: (1) plan and administer
income tax credits relating to emergency management or disaster
preparedness and grants for low-income taxpayers to fortify
their residential real property; (2) oversee the development of
emergency shelters; and (3) hire necessary personnel.
