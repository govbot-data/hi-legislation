A BILL FOR AN ACT
RELATING TO DEAF INDIVIDUALS.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAH:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that the dangers
2 associated with a routine traffic stop can increase if a law
3 enforcement officer is not aware that the driver is deaf. A
4 deaf driver's failure to respond to verbal commands or an
5 officer's misinterpretation of a deaf individual's gestures can
6 lead to undesired outcomes.
7 Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to prevent
8 miscommunication between deaf drivers and law enforcement
9 officers by providing law enforcement officers with a
10 notification that a driver is deaf when the officer conducts an
11 electronic check of the motor vehicle's registration.
12 SECTION 2. Chapter 286, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is
13 amended by adding a new section to part III to be appropriately
14 designated and to read as follows:
15 ”§286- Deaf owner; registration. (a) Upon application
16 by a motor vehicle owner who is deaf, the director of finance of
17 the county where the vehicle is to be operated shall add a
