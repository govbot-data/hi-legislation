BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAH:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that when an individual
2 is charged under both federal and state law, the federal
3 investigation and prosecution of that individual takes
4 precedence over those of the State. In the event that the
5 length or outcome of a federal investigation precludes the
6 statute of limitations for the prosecution of a crime under
7 state law, the indicted may avoid prosecution in the State,
8 resulting in a miscarriage of justice. Bribery, for example, is
9 a class B felony, and generally, state law provides for a
10 statute of limitations of three years for class B felonies.
11 Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to establish a
12 statute of limitations of nine years for a bribery offense.
13 SECTION 2. Section 710-1040, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is
14 amended to read as follows:
