STATEMENT OF OBJECTIONS TO SENATE BILL NO. 3262
Honorable Members
Thirty-Third Legislature
State of Hawai'i
Pursuant to Section 16 of Article III of the Constitution of the State of
Hawai'i, I am returning herewith, without my approval. Senate Bill No. 3262, entitled "A
Bill for an Act Relating to Education."
The purpose of this bill is to require the Hawai'i Teacher Standards Board
to submit three nominees for the Hawai'i Teacher Standards Board Executive Director
to the Board of Education and require the Board of Education to appoint the Executive
Director from among those nominees, subject to the advice and consent of the Senate.
This bill is objectionable because further review of the separation of
responsibilities and duties between the Board of Education and the Hawai'i Teacher
Standards Board is needed to assess how the proposed changes may affect the
respective roles, authorities, and oversight functions of each entity. This review will
ensure that any future changes are clearly defined, appropriately structured, and do not
create ambiguity regarding governance and accountability.
For the foregoing reasons, I am returning Senate Bill No. 3262 without my
approval.
Respectfully,
JOSH GREEN, M.D.
Governor of Hawai'i
