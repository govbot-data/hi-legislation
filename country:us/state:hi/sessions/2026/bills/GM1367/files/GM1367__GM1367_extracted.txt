A BILL FOR AN ACT

BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that Hawaii's public
2 school system is a foundational public asset that is facing
3 significant challenges. Projections from the Western Interstate
4 Commission for Higher Education indicate that Hawaii is expected
5 to experience the nation's steepest decline in high school
6 graduates over the next sixteen years, with enrollment decreases
7 likely to accelerate.
8 The legislature further finds that, while statewide public
9 school enrollment today is nearly the same as it was in 1961,
10 the number of schools has increased by approximately twenty per
11 cent. Decades of enrollment growth led to the construction of
12 new campuses, but the recent and continuing decline in student
13 numbers has left many facilities underutilized. This imbalance
14 has created operational inefficiencies and diverted resources
15 that could otherwise be used to support student learning or
16 modernize facilities in high-need areas. The legislature also
17 finds that prior efforts to consolidate or close schools have

14 The purpose of this Act is to establish a temporary,

16 review the State's public school facilities and recommend a
17 comprehensive package of consolidations, realignments, or
18 closures for an expedited, up-or-down approval process.
