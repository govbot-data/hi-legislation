1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that the information age
2 is changing how providers of public acconunodations and public
3 services communicate with their customers. Many service
4 providers use websites, software applications, and other
5 technologies to take reservations, display menus, take orders,
6 make sales, and provide product information.
7 The legislature recognizes that ready access to, and the
8 ability to use, information and communication technology is
9 essential to allow all citizens to fully engage with and enjoy
10 public goods, services, facilities, privileges, advantages, and
11 accommodations. However, the legislature is aware that some
12 websites and applications used by public accommodation providers
13 in the State are not accessible to persons with disabilities,
14 denying them full and equal access.
15 The legislature also recognizes that existing federal and
16 state laws that prohibit discrimination in public accommodations
17 based on disability could better inform providers of public

1 The legislature notes that a working draft of proposed
2 rules has been prepared for consideration by the Hawaii civil
3 rights commission.
4 Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to:
5 (1) Direct the Hawaii civil rights commission to adopt
6 rules implementing the prohibition against the use of
7 inaccessible information and communication technology,
8 consistent with the United States Department of
9 Justice Title II web accessibility framework; and
10 (2) Establish that it is an unlawful discriminatory
11 practice for a place of public accommodation to deny a
12 person with a disability full and equal enjoyment of
13 the goods, services, facilities, privileges,
14 advantages, or accommodations of a place of public
15 accommodation, or information related thereto, by
16 requiring the use of information and communication
17 technology that is not accessible to the person with a
18 disability.
