RELATING TO THE HAWAII COMMUNITY COLLEGE PROMISE PROGRAM.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that the Hawaii community
2 college promise program (Hawaii promise) was created to cover
3 the unmet direct costs of attendance for eligible students
4 enrolled at any university of Hawaii community college.
5 Existing law requires the inclusion of all grants, scholarships,
6 and other funding sources when calculating Hawaii promise
7 awards, which can reduce the benefit to students.
8 The legislature further finds that removing grants,
9 scholarships, and other funding sources from the Hawaii promise
10 award calculation will allow students to apply such funds toward
11 indirect cost needs, including housing, food, and other living
12 expenses, thereby reducing financial barriers, promoting
13 equitable access to higher education, and providing better
14 support for students in completing their academic programs.
15 The legislature finds that recent changes to the federal
16 Pell Grant require updates to Hawaii promise to ensure awarding
