BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that type two diabetes is
2 a significant driver of preventable health care costs in the
3 State, particularly in rural, neighbor island, and medically
4 underserved communities where access to consistent outpatient
5 management and specialty care is limited. Poorly controlled
6 diabetes contributes to avoidable emergency department visits,
7 hospitalizations, and long-term complications that place
8 substantial strain on public and private health care systems.
9 The legislature further finds that continuous glucose
10 monitors are an evidence-based diabetes management technology
11 that provide real-time glucose data, supports earlier clinical
12 intervention, and improves glycemic control. The use of
13 continuous glucose monitors has been shown to reduce
14 hypoglycemic events and high-cost acute care utilization, making
15 continuous glucose monitors an effective tool for improving
16 health outcomes while mitigating downstream health care
17 expenditures.

1 The legislature also finds that inconsistent insurance
2 coverage for continuous glucose monitors creates inefficiencies
3 in care delivery and increases the likelihood of higher-cost
4 interventions. Standardizing coverage across health plans,
5 including medicaid managed care programs, promotes predictable
6 benefits, reduces administrative burdens, and supports uniform
7 clinical practice.
8 Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to require all
9 health insurers in the State, including medicaid managed care
10 programs, to cover the cost of continuous glucose monitors.
