RELATING TO DEMENTIA.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that dementia and
2 cognitive impairment are growing public health concerns in
3 Hawaii, particularly among the State's aging population. The
4 impacts of dementia extend beyond individuals to families,
5 caregivers, and communities, placing emotional and financial
6 strain on households and the health care system.
7 The legislature further finds that access to early
8 diagnosis, care planning, and long-term support for individuals
9 with dementia and cognitive impairment is limited, especially in
10 rural areas and on neighbor islands. Many residents face
11 barriers to receiving timely and coordinated care due to
12 geographic isolation, workforce shortages, and fragmented
13 service delivery.
14 The need for systemic reform for dementia and cognitive
15 impairment treatment cannot be overstated. Alzheimer's disease
16 is estimated to affect one in nine Hawaii residents over age
17 sixty-five. With the older adult population in the State
