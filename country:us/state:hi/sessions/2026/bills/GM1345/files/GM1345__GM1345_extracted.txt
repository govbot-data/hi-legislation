1 SECTION 1. This Act shall be known and may be cited as the
2 Hawaii Braille Literacy Education Act.
3 SECTION 2. The legislature finds that literacy skills are
4 fundamental to success in education, employment, and life for
5 all members of society, including individuals with low vision
6 and blindness, Braille is a tactile writing system that allows
7 low vision and blind individuals to read by touch, using a
8 system of raised dots that represent letters, numbers, and
9 symbols. Braille literacy skills are widely understood as
10 essential for low vision and blind students to actively learn,
11 seek and maintain remunerative employment, engage in cultural
12 enrichment and hobbies, and access opportunities in life
13 regardless of disability.
14 According to a 2018 study reported in the Journal of
15 Blindness Innovation and Research, "individuals who were primary
16 braille readers since childhood had greater life satisfaction,
17 self-esteem, and job satisfaction than individuals who reported
