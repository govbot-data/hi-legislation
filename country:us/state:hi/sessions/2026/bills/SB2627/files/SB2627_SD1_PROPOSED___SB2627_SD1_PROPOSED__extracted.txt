THE SENATE
S.B. NO.
THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026
STATE OF HAWAII
A BILL FOR AN ACT
RELATING TO ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT AND TOURISM.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that, procurement and
2 contracting activities conducted in accordance with the State's
3 standard procurement process pursuant to chapter 103D, Hawaii
4 Revised Statutes, typically require months to complete. These
5 delays may result in missed opportunities, particularly when
6 negotiating sponsorship and partnership agreements with national
7 and international sports brands and teams and when executing
8 marketing campaigns that are time-sensitive and tied to seasonal
9 or global events.
10 The legislature further finds that allowing the Hawaii
11 tourism authority to utilize a procurement exemption for sports
12 projects and events and marketing activities will enable the
13 authority to:
14 (1) Act swiftly to secure sponsorships and partnerships
15 that enhance the State's brand visibility;

1 (2) Maintain competitiveness against other destinations
2 that can move faster in securing promotional
3 opportunities; and
4 (3) Operate efficiently by reducing administrative and
5 bureaucratic delays, allowing staff to focus on
6 implementation.
7 Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to exempt the
8 Hawaii tourism authority from the Hawaii Public Procurement Code
9 and all other requirements of law for competitive bidding for
10 contracts and agreements for sports projects and events and
11 marketing activities.
12 SECTION 2. Chapter 201B, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is
13 amended by adding a new section to part II to be appropriately
14 designated and to read as follows:
15 "§201B- Exemption of authority from Hawaii public
16 procurement code. The authority shall not be subject to chapter
17 103D and any and all other requirements of law for competitive
18 bidding for contracts or agreements for:
19 (1) Sports projects and events; and
20 (2) Marketing activities."
21 SECTION 3. New statutory material is underscored.
