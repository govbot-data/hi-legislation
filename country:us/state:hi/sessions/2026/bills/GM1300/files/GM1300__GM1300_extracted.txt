1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that, in a 2021
2 quantitative research study, the AARP found that, among Hawaii
3 residents aged forty-five and older, seventy-five per cent of
4 the respondents reported that having affordable long-term care
5 options in their communities was an important independent living
6 issue. Respondents also reported that maintaining a connection
7 with family and friends and the ability to continue visiting
8 familiar health care providers are significantly easier when the
9 State's kupuna can be cared for closer to home. The legislature
10 recognizes that, in 2026, a strong demand for community-based
11 care options for kupuna still exists.
12 The legislature further finds that the latest review of the
13 State's home and community-based medicaid reimbursement rates
14 for residential services was a third-party study conducted by
15 the actuarial firm Milliman, published on December 30, 2022.
16 The study developed benchmark comparison rates for certain home
17 and community-based services, including residential services
