A BILL FOR AN ACT
RELATING TO TRAFFIC SAFETY.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that Hawaii experienced
2 its deadliest year on the roads since 2007, with one hundred
3 twenty-nine traffic fatalities in 2025, which is a staggering
4 thirty-two per cent increase from the previous year. The
5 fatalities included forty motor vehicle occupants, thirty-nine
6 motorcycle and motor scooter riders, and thirty-eight
7 pedestrians, with over ninety per cent of these crashes
8 attributed to risky behaviors such as speeding and impairment.
9 The legislature further finds that among the lives lost was
10 19-year-old Tehya Mahelona, who was walking to her job in
11 Nanakuli when she was fatally struck in a chain-reaction crash
12 caused by speeding. A devoted sister and aspiring pilot, Tehya
13 was known for her independence, compassion, and commitment to
14 helping others - qualities that defined her as a role model in
15 her family and community.
16 Therefore, the purpose of this Act is to direct and
17 appropriate funds for the Department of Transportation to
