STATEMENT OF OBJECTIONS TO SENATE BILL NO. 2600
Honorable Members
Thirty-Third Legislature
State of Hawaii
Pursuant to Section 16 of Article III of the Constitution of the State of
Hawaii, I am returning herewith, without my approval. Senate Bill No. 2600, entitled "A
Bill for an Act Relating to the General Fund."
The purpose of this bill is to dispose of excess general fund revenues, as
required by Section 6 of Article VII of the Hawai'i Constitution, by appropriating funds to
be deposited into the Emergency and Budget Reserve Fund.
This bill is objectionable because given the State's significant fiscal
obligations, including the need to expand affordable housing and address the rising cost
of living for our residents, the transfer of $50,000,000 to the Emergency and Budget
Reserve Fund, the State's rainy day fund, designated in section 2 on page 2 of the bill,
would not be fiscally prudent. I therefore object to the transfer.
The Emergency and Budget Reserve Fund is already adequately
capitalized, with an existing balance of approximately $1,592,000,000. Considering the
Fund's current financial condition, the transfer is not necessary to ensure the Fund's
continued operations or financial stability. Retaining these general funds will preserve
the State's fiscal flexibility and strength in its ability to respond to evolving economic
conditions, unforeseen contingencies, and other critical public needs. Sound financial
management requires that limited general funds be conserved and allocated to the
State's highest priority obligations, particularly during this period of economic
uncertainty and changing fiscal demands.
Section 16 of Article III of the Constitution of the State of Hawai'i provides
that, "[ejxcept for items appropriated to be expended by the judicial and legislative
branches, the governor may veto any specific item or items in any bill which

Because of the foregoing objections, pursuant to my line-item veto authority, I have

For the foregoing reasons, I am returning Senate Bill No. 2600 with the
reduction set forth above totaling $49,999,999 in the general fund appropriation for
fiscal year 2026-2027.
