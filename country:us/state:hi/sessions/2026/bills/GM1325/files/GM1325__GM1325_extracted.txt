1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that hearing loss is a
2 significant public health concern that affects individuals of
3 all ages, from keiki to kupuna. National and state-level data
4 indicate that tens of thousands of Hawaii residents experience
5 some degree of hearing loss, including many who report serious
6 hearing difficulty or deafness. Hearing loss becomes
7 increasingly common with age, affecting roughly one-third of
8 adults in their sixties, a majority of adults in their
9 seventies, and most adults in their eighties.
10 The legislature further finds that despite the prevalence
11 of hearing loss, it remains widely underdiagnosed and
12 undertreated. Although newborn hearing screenings are in place,
13 Hawaii lacks a coordinated statewide approach to screening and
14 follow-up for school-age children and adults. Primary care
15 providers often receive limited training and reimbursement
16 incentives to screen for hearing loss, hearing aids and
17 assistive devices remain prohibitively expensive for many
