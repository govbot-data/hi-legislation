A BILL FORAN ACT
RELATING TO REAL PROPERTY.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that the State of Hawaii
2 utilizes the regular system and the land court to record legal
3 documents pertaining to the ownership of property. In general,
4 property is recorded in either the regular system or the land
5 court; however, in some circumstances property is recorded in
6 both systems. The regular system and the land court have
7 different requirements for recording documents. The land court
8 creates barriers to access in the form of delay and increased
9 expenses resulting from the complicated procedures and
10 requirements in the land court. No new properties have been
11 registered in the land court since 1989, which indicates sharp
12 public preference for the regular system.
13 The legislature further finds that the regular system is a
14 "race-notice" land registration system and does not make any
15 independent determination about the priority or ownership of
16 interests in the property. This is one of the reasons why the
17 regular system is preferred by the public. On the other hand,

1 the land court system is a "Torrens" system of land
2 registration, which means one must "race" to the land court to

5 The legislature also finds that Act 120, Session laws of
6 Hawaii 2009, amended chapter 501, Hawaii Revised Statutes, in an
7 attempt to ease the backlog in the bureau of conveyances. Among
8 other things, the amendments allowed owners of interest in land
9 registered in the land court system to transfer the interest to
10 the regular system.
11 The purpose of this Act is to simplify the land
12 registration process by repealing the land court system and
13 transitioning all recorded documents to the regular system.
