1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that numerous species in
2 the State are on the brink of extinction, in particular many
3 indigenous birds found nowhere else in the world. While the
4 State has made progress in addressing the myriad causes
5 contributing to extinction, the legislature finds that existing
6 efforts must be supplemented to prevent further loss of
7 biodiversity.
8 The legislature recognizes that Zealandia (Te Mara a Tane)
9 is a pioneering conservation sanctuary in Wellington, New
10 Zealand, best known for proving how dramatically nature can
11 rebound with proper protection. Built as a fully fenced,
12 predator-excluded sanctuary, Zealandia creates a safe haven
13 where native birds, reptiles, and plants thrive and recover in
14 near-natural habitats, safe from invasive predators and vectors.
15 This model has not only enabled the reintroduction and rapid
16 growth of iconic native species that were once on the brink of
