I SECTION 1. The legislature finds that Alzheimer's disease
2 and other forms of dementia are not only detrimental to
3 residents of the State but also affect businesses, economic
4 growth, and visitors to Hawaii.
5 Creating a dementia-friendly business environment would
6 greatly enhance the quality of life for those living with
7 dementia and their caregivers, while demonstrating that these
8 diseases are not just a health issue, but also an economic and
9 community development priority.
10 Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to establish a
11 business recognition program within the department of business,
12 economic development, and tourism to promote brain health
13 awareness in the business community and provide knowledge on the
14 harmful effects of not only Alzheimer's disease, but all forms
15 of dementia, to better serve and support customers while
16 creating a dementia-capable workforce.
