1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that waste from the use
2 of disposable electronic smoking devices poses significant
3 environmental problems. Disposable electronic smoking devices
4 are not recyclable, contain hazardous components like lithium
5 batteries and liquid nicotine, and contribute to plastic
6 pollution, landfill fires, and toxic chemical leaching into
7 waterways. According to a study conducted by Truth Initiative,
8 the nation's J^argest nonprofit public health organization
9 dedicated to preventing youth nicotine addiction, over half of
10 young people surveyed discarded their disposable electronic
11 smoking devices in the trash, with less than a quarter reporting
12 that they recycled their devices.
13 The legislature further finds that the local and global
14 impact of the world’s increasing waste stream is unsustainable
15 and detrimental to the environmental integrity of the State.
16 The exponential rise in single-use plastics and bioplastics has
17 worsened problems related to street and beach litter, ocean
